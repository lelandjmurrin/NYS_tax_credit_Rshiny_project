Summary:

This website presents the application and methodology for predicting the best estimate for Earned Tax Credit for both income and industry for New York State Article 9A by tax year.

Conclusions:

  • The industry model generates slightly more accurate predictions compared to the income model
  • The data had to be transformed in order to overcome the abnormal distribution of the amount of credit and privacy issues resulting in missing values
  • The model explains approximately 70% of the variability in the test data when using a train/test split of 80:20

Limitations:

  • The model accuracy could be improved, but for now it can be used for signal comparison (Can we expect credit to go up or down across years?)
  • There are some industries, income groups and credit names taken out by the best model,
    but those fields generally should remain at the same levels based on Number of Taxpayers and Tax Year
  • Credit Earned was considered over other Credit Types (e.g. Credit Carried Forward) because they make the data more standardized (i.e. the Amount of Credit doesn't accumulate across years)

Recommendations:

  • Analyze how clients tax credits earned under Article 9A compare to other income levels and industries
  • Assist in identifying potential new clients based on industry and or income level
  • Give a rough estimate of what clients should be earning in this tax year and subsequent tax years
